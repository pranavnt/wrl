# Q-OIL transport sweep findings (v3‚Äìv5)

Hyperparameter study of the two Q-OIL knobs on robomimic **transport** (pixels),
residual on the frozen 34% Diffusion Policy base. `edit_scale` fixed at the
v1/v2 winner **0.15** throughout. Metric is **best eval success** (max over the
run's eval trajectory; note this is upward-biased, see caveats). Base DP ‚âà 34%.

Generated by `hyak/gen_configs_v{3,4,5}.py`; analyzed with
`hyak/analyze_sweep.py <group> 33000` (the `33000` done-bar matters ‚Äî runs cap at
the 34k-step budget, so the default 40k bar marks everything "in progress").

## The two knobs

- **`bc_weight`** ‚Äî weight on BC regularization that anchors the residual to the
  expert/intervention actions (dense, supervised, on every intervention chunk).
- **`intervention_bonus`** ‚Äî optimism reward added *only* to the decoupled
  optimistic critic `Q_Opt`, *only* on intervention transitions. The task reward
  itself is untouched (sparse 0/1 success); `Q_Opt` bootstraps from the clean
  task critic `Q_TD`, so the bonus stays local to intervention states.

## Results

### v4 ‚Äî full grid (bc √ó bonus, 3 seeds, 54 configs)

mean best eval, `bonus` across, `bc_weight` down:

```
        b0     b0.4   b0.8   b1.2   b1.6   b2.0
bc0.0   3%     0%     0%     0%     5%     2%     <- collapses
bc0.1   52%    53%    52%    53%    60%    53%    <- flat & high
bc0.25  42%    57%    53%    43%    38%    28%    <- inverted-U
```

### v5 ‚Äî low-optimism zoom (bc √ó small bonus, 3 seeds, 24 configs)

```
        b0.025 b0.05  b0.1   b0.2
bc0.1   53%    50%    45%    50%
bc0.25  50%    52%    60%    45%
```

## Conclusions

1. **`bc_weight` is the dominant axis, and `bc=0` is fatal.** With no BC anchor
   the residual collapses to ~0‚Äì5% (pure sparse-reward RL on this task can't
   learn). `bc>0` is mandatory; `0.1` vs `0.25` is roughly a wash.

2. **Optimism is a broad flat shoulder, then a cliff.** From `bonus ‚âà 0.025` to
   `0.4`, performance is flat at ~50‚Äì57% ‚Äî the magnitude barely matters. Above
   ~0.4 it decays, sharply at high BC (`bc=0.25`: 57% @ 0.4 ‚Üí 28% @ 2.0). There
   is **no tuned optimum at 0.4** ‚Äî it's a plateau edge, not a peak.

3. **Why large bonus hurts:** the task reward is sparse (0 on nearly every
   chunk), so a per-intervention bonus of 1‚Äì2 is comparable to or larger than
   the actual task signal and swamps `Q_Opt`. Small bonus is a gentle nudge;
   large bonus overrides the objective.

4. **v3 was wrong.** v3's "more bonus ‚Üí monotonically better, b=2.0 best" was a
   small-sample / immature-baseline artifact (its `bonus=0` runs hadn't matured)
   and is contradicted by v4/v5.

**Operating point:** `edit_scale=0.15`, `bc_weight ‚àà {0.1, 0.25}`,
`bonus ‚àà {0.05, 0.4}`. Selected for the YAM transfer: **`bc=0.25`, `bonus=0.1`**
(see [yam_port.md](yam_port.md)).

## Caveats

- `best_eval` is max-over-trajectory ‚Üí upward-biased; `final_eval` (in the CSV)
  is the less flattering number.
- 3 seeds cannot resolve the ~5‚Äì10pp gaps between flat cells; cell-to-cell
  wiggles within the plateau are seed noise, not signal. Separating them needs
  more seeds, not finer grid.
- v4 readout is at 39/54 done; v5 at 24/24 done.
